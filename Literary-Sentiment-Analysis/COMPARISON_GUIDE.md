## ğŸ“Š What to Compare

### Sentiment Analysis
- **Overall sentiment:** Positive/Negative/Neutral
- **Confidence scores:** Which text is more definitively positive or negative?
- **Word counts:** Positive vs negative word frequency

### Emotional Content
- **Primary emotions:** What's the dominant emotion in each?
- **Emotion distribution:** Compare the 7-emotion breakdown
- **Emotional intensity:** Which has stronger emotional content?

### Literary Elements
- **Devices used:** Which uses more metaphors, imagery, etc.?
- **Themes:** Are themes similar or different?
- **Tone:** Compare formality, intensity, pacing

### Readability
- **Grade level:** Which is more complex?
- **Sentence structure:** Longer or shorter sentences?
- **Vocabulary:** Lexical diversity percentage

### Genre & Style
- **Classification:** Both poetry? One narrative?
- **Structure:** Line count, word count differences

## ğŸ’¡ Example Comparisons

### Example 1: Comparing Two Poets

**Text 1: Robert Frost**
```
The woods are lovely, dark and deep,
But I have promises to keep,
And miles to go before I sleep.
```

**Text 2: Emily Dickinson**
```
Hope is the thing with feathers
That perches in the soul,
And sings the tune without the words.
```

**Compare:**
- Frost: **Contemplative, duty-focused** (themes: duty, journey)
- Dickinson: **Hopeful, optimistic** (themes: hope, nature)
- Frost: More **complex syntax**, higher grade level
- Dickinson: Simpler language, **central metaphor**
- Frost: Neutral-to-melancholic sentiment
- Dickinson: Positive sentiment (95%)

- **Cultural nuances:** Spanish may score higher on emotion
